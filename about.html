About Atelier Cygne

A studio for looking
at rooms slowly.

Atelier Cygne began as a notebook of things worth noticing in the built world — a doorway, a stair rail, the way light sits in a stairwell at four in the afternoon. It became a monthly journal because some ideas need a whole edition to say properly.

One

One Edition a Month

No daily feed, no scroll. A single, considered edition is published each month, meant to be read in one sitting or returned to across a week.

Two

Rooms Over Trends

We write about how spaces are actually inhabited — light, material, proportion, the way a house ages — rather than what's fashionable this season.

Three

Design & Architecture, Together

The line between a building and the rooms inside it is mostly artificial. We cover both, because a chair and a stairwell are answering the same question.
