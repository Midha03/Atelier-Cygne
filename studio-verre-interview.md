Studio Verre's interiors are recognisable mostly for what isn't there — no visible hardware, no decorative moulding, almost no colour beyond the material itself. We spoke with the studio about why subtraction has become their signature move.

**On starting a project:** "We usually begin by asking what the room absolutely needs, not what it could have. Most of design is knowing what to leave out."

**On material honesty:** "If we use plaster, we want you to know it's plaster. Hiding what something is made of is usually where a room starts to feel dishonest."

**On working with clients:** "The hardest part of the job is convincing someone that the empty wall is the design decision, not the absence of one."

The studio's approach won't suit every room or every client — but it's a useful discipline to borrow from even in small doses: before adding something to a space, ask what could be removed instead.
