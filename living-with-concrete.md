Concrete's reputation in residential interiors is mostly inherited from its public buildings — car parks, underpasses, civic blocks built fast and cheap. That association is doing the material a disservice.

Left exposed and finished well, concrete has a warmth that photographs rarely capture: a soft, chalky surface that changes tone through the day exactly like the light-sensitive materials we tend to praise more, like limewash or raw plaster.

## Making it work at home

The interiors that succeed with concrete almost always pair it with something that absorbs sound and softens edges — timber, wool, linen — so the room doesn't read as cold, only quiet. Underfloor heating helps too, solving concrete's other common complaint before it becomes one.

Used this way, concrete stops being a style statement and starts being simply another honest material in the room, doing exactly what it's good at.
